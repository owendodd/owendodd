Artsy City Guide

The Artsy City Guide was created to enable our user-base to easily discover and visit art in the physical world. The digital experience allows users to follow artists, galleries and museums for updates on new shows, and to navigate these shows in our 6 launch cities. The product was built as part of an on-going partnership with BMW and was integrated into our existing iOS app.

Background

The Artsy iOS app has existed since 2015 as a tool for art enthusiasts to browse and save artworks, follow artists, and explore fairs, galleries and museums. The app experience was ultimately aimed at enabling collectors to purchase works of art via conversations with galleries and by bidding in auctions.

Opportunity definition

The Artsy iOS app has consistently been one of the primary drivers of commercial activity but was falling short of retention and engagement goals. Previous collector research had indicated that while most collectors don't usually purchase works highly regular basis, they are consistently visiting galleries, museums and fairs.

After completing an in-depth competitor analysis, we saw a large opportunity to create a product with a more seamless and comprehensive user-experience, leveraging both our existing commercial infrastructure and personalization layers, neither of which existed in competitive products.

These inputs led us to believe that creating a product focused on the exploration of shows and fairs could increase our app engagement while simultaneously enhancing collectors commercial experience.

Research

To further understand the specifics of we conducted a series of user interviews. Each conversation was focused on highlighting the relationship between a collectors commercial behavior and their show visiting habits. With each subject we discussed what factors influenced their decisions to visit (or not visit) a show or fair, what key information they rely on, and what tools they use to assist throughout their process.

These conversations reinforced some of our initial assumptions around the importance of personalization and of robust information on the artists, galleries, and museums associated with shows. They also highlighted the importance of social validation and reccomendations throughout the show discovery process, which will be explored in subsequent iterations of the app.

Exploration and iteration

After developing an initial understanding of our opportunity with key stakeholders, we began developing the information architecture and structure of the app through a series of wireframes. These explorations were continuously assessed by our internal teams and tweaked based on feedback.

Simultaneously we began to explore a number of key screens, and iterate on various components. These explorations were then evaluated by internal teams and reconciled with our existing UX patterns and visual language.

Design

The core app experience is based around 6 initial launch locations. When location services are enabled for users within these cities, they are automatically placed in their respective city view, with the ability to quickly switch to other locations via the dropdown menu. The feature set will quickly be expanded to support wider geographic areas as we scale.

The home view of each city offers a variety of entry points into the wide range of shows and fairs, organized into time sensitive, personalized, and curated content buckets.

The map view for each city allows users to quickly place themselves in relation to the events around them. Specific UI considerations were implemented to allow for easy browsing of multiple events in dense areas.

New fair views allow users to browse and search by artists, exhibitors (galleries), or works. Additional information about the fair location and other programming is also available on the overview page.

The show view surfaces installation shots and works included in the show, along with additional information provided by the gallery. This view is integrated with our existing artist and artwork infrastructure.

Analysis

Preliminary results since our launch of the app have surpassed initial goals for account creation and have resulted in the highest number of monthly commercial interactions within the app to date.
