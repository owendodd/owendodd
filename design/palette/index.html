Palette Design System

Background

Over the past 3 years, the Artsy product team has taken various attempts at developing a cohesive design system to enable quicker and more consistent iteration across our products. Without a consistent approach, these initiatives have been limited in scope to specific product areas and have failed to gain traction within the organization.

Working without dedicated design systems resources, we knew we must focus on creating a system that could be developed in a distributed manner and whose work could be incorporated into existing product teams work as much as possible.

Systems audit

Assessing our existing implementation of common UI and UX patterns revealed a high level of inconsistency in both the design treatment and implementation approach to functionally similar components.

As our codebase moved more and more into React, our usage of components continued to be team specific, and lacked a cohesive strategy. These divergent approachs resulted in a large amount of overhead in developing new experiences and iterating on current products.

Designing process

With the goal of creating a system that was easily consumable by a variety of teams working both on design and engineering, we focused our attention on first articulating a lightweight and easily replicable process for any potential contributors. The result of this was the formation of a small task force, meeting on a biweekly basis, and robust documentation for all aspects of the design systems development process.

Our documentation was created to ensure a consistent style and framework for all additions to our design system. This was especially important for our team as both the engineering and design team scaled without dedicated resources focused on the health and continued application of these systems.

System heirarchy

The most fundimental problem for our task force to address was the question of what should be included in a cohesive design system. Creating a hierarchical vocabularly allowed us to assess all potential additions to the code base based on their specificity of application and their scalability accross our product experiences. All additions to this system should be usefull across these products and should work in a wide range contexts and for a variety of users.
