Palette Design System

Background

Over the past 3 years, the Artsy design team has taken various attempts at developing a cohesive design system to enable quicker and more consistent iteration across our products. Without a structured approach, these initiatives have been limited in scope to specific product areas and have failed to gain traction across the organization.

Working without dedicated design systems resources, we knew we must focus on creating a system that could be developed in a distributed manner and whose work could be incorporated into existing product team roadmaps as much as possible.

Systems audit

Assessing our existing implementation of common UI and UX patterns revealed a high level of inconsistency in both the design treatment and technical implementation of functionally similar components.

As our codebase moved more and more into React, our usage of components continued to be team specific, and lacked a cohesive strategy. These divergent approaches resulted in a large amount of overhead in developing new experiences and iterating on current products.

Designing process

With the goal of creating a system that was easily consumable by both design and engineering teams, we focused our attention first on articulating a lightweight and easily replicable process for any potential contributors. The result of this was the formation of a small task force, meeting on a biweekly basis, and robust documentation for all aspects of the design systems development process.

Our documentation was created to ensure a consistent style and framework for all additions to our design system. This was especially important as our product team rapidly scaled while maintain a singular approach.

System heirarchy

The most fundamental problem for our task force to address was defining what should and should not be included in the design system. Creating a hierarchical vocabulary allowed us to assess all potential additions to the Palette code base based on their specificity of application and their scalability across our product experiences. All additions to this system needed to be applicable to a wide range of products and device types.

Components: typography

After an initial round of standardization of our type sizes, we further refined our type system by creating an additional layer of typographic variables. The semantic naming of these variables created more clarity in how they should be applied. Additionally, responsive logic was built into each variable, allowing us to more easily scale type relationships across our breakpoints.

Throughout this process, it was crucial to continually test these variables across our key product surfaces to ensure their functionality.

Components: quick inputs

Our quick edit inputs were created to capture information as seamlessly as possible, and are prominently used in our log in and sign up flow.

Components: modals

Modals are used across our platform for quick actions and to surface important contextual information. The new modal component in Palette was created with two size variations, and with a number of standardized treatment options for CTAs, forms, titles and other commonly used UI.

Components: data visualization

The line chart and donut chart components were built on top of our existing bar chart data visualization component to support a wider range of data types. These components are used both on the front end of the site, and in our partner CMS to show analytics and market data.

Moving forward

As our design system is increasingly adopted across in more parts of the product, we are continuing to develop and consolidate our documentation, and useage guidelines, with the goal of making this work as easily accessible as possible across the team. This work will continue to evolve and be refined as we increase usage and find the boundaries of our current systems.
